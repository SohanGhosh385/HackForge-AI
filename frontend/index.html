Resource Guide

Evaluation Guide

How HackForge AI analyzes, scores, and refines hackathon project concepts.

01. Dynamic Calibration

HackForge AI adjusts the difficulty calibration based on your team's hacking duration and background:

  • Beginner (24h-48h): Serverless stacks, pre-built API integrations, and single-pane UI templates to guarantee a working demo.
  • Intermediate (48h-72h): Structured databases, basic background workers, and modular design patterns.
  • Advanced (72h+ / Weeks): Microservices, queue systems, custom ML model tuning, and production-grade architectures.

02. Feasibility Check

The AI recommendation engine validates your choice of technology against current production standards, library compatibilities, and learning curves, ensuring you don't pick libraries that are deprecated or excessively difficult to boot up in a high-pressure weekend environment.

03. Blueprint Validation

Every generated idea undergoes a structural breakdown into three layers: Frontend client console, backend orchestration APIs, and storage databases/caches. This ensures your project is scalable, structured, and easy to present to judges.

Judging Matrix

Judging Criteria

Standard hackathon scoring dimensions modeled by our evaluation engine.

01. Execution 30%

Judges evaluate the depth of technical execution, correct choice of tools, architecture cleanliness, and how much functional code was actually built. Strive for a robust integration over simple mockups.

02. Demo Impact 25%

Can this project be built in the given timeframe? Does it solve a real pain point, and is the solution demo-ready? Our engine evaluates this through standard complexity vs. feasibility algorithms.

03. Innovation 25%

Originality of the concept, novelty of the GenAI integrations, and creative problem-solving approach. Avoid carbon-copy ideas and focus on unique angles.

04. Design & UX 20%

Clean visual interface, responsive layout, clear user flows, and general aesthetic appeal. A polished design greatly increases user engagement and judge favorability.
